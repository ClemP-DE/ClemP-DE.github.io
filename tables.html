Number Theory

Adventures with Number Tables

A grandson's arithmetic question, a dream, and a diagonal. When sequential numbers collected from the number line are arranged into a table, something quietly remarkable emerges: every pair of neighbours along the left-leaning diagonal differs by exactly 11. This paper explores what that simple observation implies.

The Key Observation

Sequential numbers that always differ by 11

Write out the numbers 0–9. Collect each pair of adjacent digits and you get a series of two-digit numbers. Arrange those numbers on a 10-column table — and they fall on a diagonal. The gap between each consecutive member of that diagonal is always the same:

The series
01  —  12  —  23  —  34  —  45  —  56  —  67  —  78  —  89

Each term exceeds the previous by exactly 11. The paper examines why this is so, what happens along the other diagonals and verticals of the table, and how the same logic extends to tables of any column width.
