Trajectory Diagram

collatz_trajectory_diagram.html

Visualise a single Collatz sequence as a colour-coded arrow-path chart, showing how the sequence moves between the two rules before reaching 1.

What it does

Enter any positive integer as a starting seed and the tool plots its complete Collatz sequence two ways: as a step-by-step line chart (value at each step) and as an arrow-path diagram in phase space, where each arrow connects a value to the next. Arrows are colour-coded by which rule was applied — red for odd steps (3x+1), blue for even steps (x÷2).

Controls
FieldDefault / RangeWhat it controls
Start xDefault: 3 — any positive integerThe seed value to trace
Go—Compute and plot the sequence for the current seed
Random—Pick a random seed between 2 and 1000
Clear—Remove all traces and reset the display
Reading the output
  • The summary panel shows starting value, total steps, peak value, and a count of odd vs even steps.
  • The sequence bar shows every value in order. Red numbers are odd; blue numbers are even; the final 1 is yellow.
  • In the arrow-path panel, each arrow connects a value to the next. Red arrows = odd step (3x+1); blue arrows = even step (x÷2).
  • The list of sequence points at the bottom gives the exact (x, y) coordinates of every arrow.
Tip: Try seed 27 to see a sequence that climbs dramatically before descending. Compare with a power of 2 (e.g. 16) to contrast a long winding path with a clean straight descent.
