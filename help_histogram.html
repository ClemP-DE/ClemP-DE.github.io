Steps Histogram

collatz_histogram.html

Plot the frequency distribution of stopping times — how many steps each seed in a range takes to reach 1.

What it does

Computes the number of steps to reach 1 for every seed in a chosen range, then plots a histogram showing how often each step-count (or step-count range) appears. Useful for spotting the shape of the distribution and finding which step counts are most common.

Controls
FieldDefault / RangeWhat it controls
From seedDefault: 1First seed in the range
To seedDefault: 100Last seed in the range
Bin sizeDefault: 5, range 1–50Width of each histogram bar in steps
Y-axis capDefault: 100Maximum displayed bar height; taller bars show their true count as an annotation above
Generate—Compute and draw the histogram
Clear—Remove the chart
Reading the output
  • Each bar represents a range of step-counts (e.g. 30–34 steps if bin size is 5). Its height shows how many seeds required that many steps.
  • Bars are colour-graded from blue (low count) through orange (high count).
  • Yellow bars have been capped at the Y-axis limit. The true count appears in a small annotation above the bar.
  • The summary panel reports min, max, mean, and median step counts, and the number of capped bars.
Tip: Set bin size to 1 for an exact count per step value, or increase it to smooth the distribution. Raise the Y-axis cap if many bars are being capped. Try seeds 1–1000 to see the characteristic two-peak structure.
