Steps vs Even/Odd Ratio

collatz_steps_vs_ratio.html

Scatter plot revealing how the even/odd step ratio narrows as sequences get longer.

What it does

Plots each seed as a dot on a scatter chart. The horizontal axis shows how many total steps the sequence takes to reach 1; the vertical axis shows the even÷odd ratio for that seed. The chart auto-loads with seeds 1–200 when first opened.

Controls
FieldDefault / RangeWhat it controls
From seedDefault: 1First seed in the range
To seedDefault: 200, max 5000Last seed in the range
Plot—Recompute and redraw the chart
Reading the output
  • Each dot is one seed. Hover over any dot to see the seed number, total steps, odd/even counts, ratio, and peak value.
  • Dots are colour-graded from blue (few steps) to red (many steps).
  • Horizontal dashed reference lines are drawn at even÷odd ratios of 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, and 2.5.
  • The summary panel shows average and maximum step counts and the range of ratios plotted.
Tip: Increase the seed range to 5000 to see the full cloud of points. Note how the ratio tends to cluster around 1.5–2.0 regardless of how many steps a sequence takes — a subtle signature of the conjecture.
